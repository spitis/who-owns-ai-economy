# Who bears the levy and who collects it

Figure 3 assigns annual collections according to where firms sell. Investors
bear the initial markdown according to the equity they own when the levy
becomes credible. This note estimates the resulting difference for the United
States. The Figure 3 allocation uses only sales destinations.

## Ownership anchors

Two public aggregates provide the U.S. side of the calculation:

- Foreign investors held 18.4% of U.S. corporate equity in 2025 Q4: $20.455
  trillion out of $111.355 trillion in the Federal Reserve's [Financial
  Accounts, table L.224](https://www.federalreserve.gov/releases/z1/20260319/html/l224.htm).
- U.S. residents held $12.1 trillion of foreign equity at the end of 2024,
  according to the Treasury's [survey of U.S. portfolio holdings of foreign
  securities](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0325).

Applying the first percentage to the model's $62.2 trillion U.S. listed value
gives about $50.8 trillion held by U.S. residents. Adding the $12.1 trillion
foreign portfolio gives about $62.9 trillion, or roughly half of the model's
$126.7 trillion world total. This is a rough estimate because the sources use
different definitions and dates.

## U.S. ledger

Americans are assigned 81.6% of the charge on U.S. firms. Their $12.1 trillion
of foreign equity equals 18.8% of the model's $64.5 trillion foreign base, so
they are also assigned 18.8% of the charge on foreign firms. Collections come
from the Figure 3 simulation. The amounts are annual levy equivalents at the
1% floor, in billions of dollars:

| Club stage | US collects | Americans bear | US net |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| US only | 960 | 571 | +389 |
| US + EU + UK + JKT | 655 | 609 | +46 |
| Global | 473 | 629 | -156 |

The burden would arrive principally as a one-time markdown when the levy became
credible. The annual equivalents make it comparable with collections. They
show a large U.S. surplus while the club is partial, near balance in the
published club, and a net contribution under global membership.

No allocation adjustment is included in the simulation. An ownership-based
division of collections would track the markdown more closely, but the data
needed to administer it do not exist at a consistent global scale. Holdings
through funds, custodians, and financial centers also obscure the residence of
the beneficial owner. The essay discusses a temporary founder's credit funded
from the residual collected while the club remains partial.

## Limits of the ownership data

Comparable ownership figures are incomplete for several of the model's
buckets, and holdings through funds and custodians are often attributed to the
intermediary's jurisdiction. A full ownership matrix would therefore require
substantially more judgment than the sales matrix. The available country data,
the limits of the IMF's bilateral portfolio statistics, and the relevant
research are collected in [`SOURCES.md`](SOURCES.md#ownership-data).
